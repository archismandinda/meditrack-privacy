MediTrack — Privacy Policy

Last Updated: 28 May 2025

1. What Data the App Collects

MediTrack does not collect personal data in the traditional sense. The information you enter — medicine names, dosages, stock quantities, user profile names, and symptom queries — is entered voluntarily by you for your own use. The app does not require you to provide your real name, email address, phone number, or any account credentials at any point.

The only information that is transmitted outside your device is the text you type into the AI symptom assistant and the text extracted from medicine photos when you use the AI scanning feature. This text is sent to the Anthropic API to generate a response and is described further in Section 6.

2. How Data Is Stored

All data generated within MediTrack — including medicine lists, dosage logs, stock quantities, user profiles, and any prescription images you save — is stored exclusively in local storage on your Android device. This data is never uploaded to any server, cloud service, or third-party platform. It is subject to Android's standard application sandboxing, meaning other apps on your device cannot access it. If you uninstall MediTrack, all locally stored data is permanently deleted from your device.

3. What Leaves the Device

The only data that leaves your device is:

  • AI assistant queries: When you describe symptoms or ask for medicine information in the AI assistant, the text of your query is sent to the Anthropic API over an encrypted HTTPS connection.
  • Medicine scan text: When you photograph a medicine box, prescription, or receipt, the text extracted from the image (not the image itself) may be sent to the Anthropic API to identify medicine details.

This data is used solely to generate an immediate response to your request. MediTrack does not store, log, or share this data with any party other than Anthropic for the purpose of processing your query. We encourage you to review Anthropic's own privacy policy at anthropic.com/privacy for details on how they handle API inputs.

4. Camera and Image Usage

MediTrack requests access to your device camera solely to allow you to photograph medicine boxes, prescriptions, and receipts for AI-assisted data entry, and to capture prescription images you wish to save to a medicine record. The app does not access your camera at any other time, does not record video, and does not upload photos to any server. Prescription images you save are stored locally on your device within MediTrack's private application storage.

5. Notifications

MediTrack uses Android's local notification system to alert you when only one unit of a medicine remains. These notifications are generated and delivered entirely on your device. No notification server, push messaging service, or internet connection is involved in sending these alerts. Your notification preferences can be managed at any time through your device's Settings.

6. Third-Party Services

MediTrack uses one third-party service:

Anthropic API — Used to power the AI medicine scanning feature and the AI symptom assistant. Text queries are sent to Anthropic's servers over HTTPS for processing. Anthropic is bound by its own privacy policy available at anthropic.com/privacy. MediTrack does not share any other user data with Anthropic or any other third party. No advertising SDKs, analytics SDKs, or social media SDKs are included in the app.

7. Children's Privacy

MediTrack is not directed at children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect any personal information from children. Since the app does not collect account information or personal data from any user, there is no specific data pertaining to children stored on our servers. All data remains on the user's device under the control of the device owner. If you are a parent or guardian and have concerns about your child's use of this app, please contact us at the address below.
